memento.sky

privacy policy

last updated · 7 july 2026

Memento.sky ("Memento") is a private journal of the people you've met. This policy explains what data the app touches, where it lives, and what we can and cannot see.

who we are

Memento is built by an indie developer. Contact: team.slowpress+memento@gmail.com.

what's stored on your device and in your icloud

The diary itself never leaves your devices and your private iCloud account. Specifically:

  • People you log — names, the moment-of-meeting timestamp, the mascot pick, optional photos, optional sketches.
  • Moments you attach to those people — text and timestamps.
  • Linked contacts — if you choose to link one of your address-book contacts to a person, the details you pick (such as a name or phone number) are copied into your diary. Contact linking uses Apple's out-of-process contact picker; the app never reads your address book in the background.

This data syncs only through Apple's CloudKit private database tied to your Apple ID. We have no servers and no access to it. If you enable Apple's Advanced Data Protection in iCloud settings, your data is end-to-end encrypted such that not even Apple can read it.

what we collect off your device

Three small streams, each described and reversible:

  • Anonymous usage analytics (PostHog) — off unless you turn it on. Screen views and a small set of key actions (for example first_spot_saved, paywall_shown). No names, no diary content, no photos. No autocapture. Analytics is opt-in everywhere: nothing is sent unless you enable "share anonymous usage" in settings → about → privacy.
  • Subscription state (Apple StoreKit + RevenueCat). When you subscribe or restore, Apple processes the payment; RevenueCat, our subscription infrastructure, records the subscription state and outcome under an anonymous identifier. No card data — Apple never shares it with us.
  • Diagnostics (Apple MetricKit). Crash and performance reports, aggregated by Apple and delivered to the app's local cache only. Never uploaded by us.

why

  • The diary stays on your device because it's yours.
  • Analytics — only if you opt in — tells us what to improve.
  • Subscription processing keeps the lights on.
  • Diagnostics keeps the app from crashing in your hand.

legal basis (gdpr)

  • Consent — analytics.
  • Contract performance — subscription.
  • Legitimate interest — fraud and crash detection.

sharing

  • Apple — controller for the App Store, iCloud, and StoreKit. Apple's privacy policy applies to those.
  • PostHog — processor, EU region — only if you opt in to analytics.
  • RevenueCat — processor, US region.

We never sell data, and there is no advertising or cross-app tracking of any kind.

retention

  • On-device + iCloud — until you delete (settings → about → delete everything).
  • PostHog — 12 months rolling, if you opted in.
  • RevenueCat — per their retention policy.
  • Apple receipts — per Apple and tax law.

your rights (gdpr / similar)

  • Access & portability — settings → your sky → export your sky produces a ZIP with everything: JSON, photos, and scribbles, machine-readable.
  • Erase — settings → about → delete everything wipes local and cloud copies. It is irreversible.
  • Withdraw consent — turn off "share anonymous usage" in settings → about → privacy.
  • Object / restrict — write to team.slowpress+memento@gmail.com and we'll honor it.

children

Memento is intended for ages 13 and up. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 13.

security

  • iCloud at-rest encryption (Apple-held keys) by default.
  • End-to-end encryption when you enable iCloud's Advanced Data Protection.
  • TLS in transit for analytics and subscription telemetry.
  • No Memento-side servers ever handle your diary data.

changes

This policy is versioned in our public repository (memento-legal). Changes ship with a new "last updated" date.

complaints

You can complain to us first (team.slowpress+memento@gmail.com), or directly to your local data-protection authority — for EEA / UK users, your national regulator (such as the ICO) is the typical authority.
